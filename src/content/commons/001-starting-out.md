Every project starts somewhere, and this one starts with a question I haven't fully answered yet: what, exactly, is a civic platform?

I have a rough working definition. A civic platform is a digital space that:

- helps people coordinate around shared resources, decisions, or concerns
- is oriented toward a community (a neighbourhood, a city, a commons of some kind) rather than commercial interests
- gives participants meaningful agency rather than just "engagement"

But that's the minimum bar. I want to build something that goes further — something that makes collective decision-making feel less like filling in forms and more like a genuine conversation that leads somewhere.

## Why this, why now?

I've spent a lot of time working with data that describes how institutions and communities interact — or fail to. The gap between the formal machinery of civic life (councils, committees, consultations) and how people actually experience it is striking. There's enormous energy and goodwill that doesn't find useful channels.

Part of what I want to do with this project is figure out what a better channel looks like.

## What this blog is

This is a thinking-out-loud log. I'll document decisions, dead ends, things I've borrowed or built, and things I've changed my mind about. I don't know how the project will turn out. That's kind of the point.
